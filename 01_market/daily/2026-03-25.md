## 1. Market Overview
Today’s market setup continues to be driven by the interaction between relief from geopolitical stress, oil repricing, and the question of whether risk appetite can stabilize beyond a single rebound session. The important short-term question is whether recent upside follow-through confirms a broader risk-on shift or whether the move remains a fragile relief bounce.

The most useful framing for today is not simply whether the indexes were up or down, but whether leadership stayed in higher-beta quality growth and consumer-sensitive groups, or rotated back toward defensives and energy.

## 2. Independent Interpretation of the Current Setup
The market is trying to transition from short-term panic unwinding into a more durable, selective rebound phase. That is constructive, but not yet fully proven.

The key structure remains:
- energy/oil panic eased
- broad equity sentiment improved
- investors became more willing to rotate back into growth and cyclical participation
- but macro confirmation is still required for the rebound to become durable

That means the most useful short-term positioning remains selective rather than aggressive.

## 3. Sector Trend Ranking (next 1 week)

This remains the most attractive near-term group because it benefits from improving risk appetite while still offering stronger business quality than speculative growth.

Preferred characteristics:

### Tier 2 — Consumer Discretionary
Consumer discretionary remains attractive on a relief / re-risking basis. If inflation pressure moderates and oil remains less threatening, this sector can continue to outperform in the short run.

### Watchlist / tactical downgrade — Energy
Energy is still strategically important, but the short-term setup is weaker than during peak geopolitical fear. If oil stabilizes lower, the urgency of the energy trade declines.
