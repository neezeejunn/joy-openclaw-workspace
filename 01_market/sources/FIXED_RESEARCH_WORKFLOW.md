# Fixed Research Workflow

## Goal
Use the currently readable sources in a repeatable order so daily reports are more consistent and less dependent on ad hoc searching.

## Current Core Source Stack
1. CNBC
2. Morningstar
3. Barron's market-data pages
4. YouTube channel comparison layer (rhinofinance / 视野环球财经)

## Workflow Order
### Step 1: Macro and market structure
Primary source: CNBC
Read:
- market live update / daily open type article
- one same-day macro market article if available

Questions to answer:
- Is the market risk-on, neutral, or risk-off?
- What is the key macro driver today?
- Is there a clear stress factor (rates, oil, geopolitics, earnings, regulation)?

### Step 2: Style, valuation, and sector framing
Primary source: Morningstar
Read:
- market overview / sectors / featured coverage
- valuation or market-structure notes when available

Questions to answer:
- Which styles or sectors are strongest / weakest?
- What does the 1M / 3M backdrop suggest?
- Is the market cheap, expensive, or mixed by style/sector?

### Step 3: Market-data and calendar context
Primary source: Barron's market-data pages
Read:
- market-data home / futures / calendars / economy items
- any directly relevant linked market article if readable

Questions to answer:
- What events or reports matter next?
- Is there near-term macro/event risk that may affect sector ranking?

### Step 4: Stock and company layer
Primary sources:
- CNBC company/fundamental stories
- Morningstar stock-focused pieces

Questions to answer:
- Which stocks best express the preferred sector view?
- What is the fundamental reason to prefer these names now?
- Are there signs the market is rewarding quality, cash flow, or capital discipline?

### Step 5: Technical overlay
Use source commentary plus any clearly stated levels or trend breaks.

Questions to answer:
- Are the broad indexes above or below key trend levels?
- Which sector ETFs or benchmark groups are strongest / weakest?
- Are there obvious invalidation levels to watch?

### Step 6: YouTube comparison layer
Target channel:
- rhinofinance / 视野环球财经

Rule:
- compare only after independent analysis is complete
- use same-day content only for formal comparison reports

Questions to answer:
- Where do we agree?
- Where do we disagree?
- Is the channel more index-focused while our process is more allocation-focused?

## Final Output Structure
1. Facts from source reading
2. Independent interpretation
3. Sector ranking
4. Stock ranking
5. Technical/fundamental notes
6. Channel comparison
7. Confidence and caveats
