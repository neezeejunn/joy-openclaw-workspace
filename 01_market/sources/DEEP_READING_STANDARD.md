# Deep Reading Standard

## Principle
Do not rely only on homepage headlines when producing market analysis.
At least several source articles should be opened and read for substance before forming conclusions.

## Minimum Standard
For a serious market report, aim to:
1. Read multiple articles, not just titles
2. Extract actual facts, not only headline framing
3. Separate what is reported from what is inferred
4. Use article content to support sector and stock conclusions

## Preferred Reading Layers
- Macro / market structure article
- Sector-specific article
- Company/fundamental article
- Optional technical or strategy note

## Reporting Rule
The report should clearly distinguish:
- Facts from source articles
- Independent interpretation
- Sector ranking
- Stock ranking
- Comparison with external commentary

## Constraint
If only shallow title-level information is available, the report should be marked as lower-confidence.
